Welcome to PCCOE Art Circle

Objective

To promote cultural activities among students and to provide platform to students to show case there various arts / skills / talents.

Functions

  1. To plan, coordinate and control cultural activities of the students.
  2. To guide students to participate in various competitions.
  3. To provide proper guidance to students through experts to enhance there skills/arts for overall development.
  4. To arrange for providing essential facilities to the students for preparation and practice.
  5. Monitor and maintain utmost discipline during practice, preparation and conduct of the events.

Painting

Painting is the expression of ideas and emotions, with the creation of certain aesthetic qualities, in a two-dimensional visual language.

Singing

Singing is the act of creating musical sounds with the voice. A person who sings is called a singer, artist or vocalist (in jazz and/or popular music).

Anchoring

Anchoring involves orchestrating the flow of the events, holding the attention of the audience with wit , humour and smart use of crisp and well modulated speech.

Drama

A drama is a type of narrative writing that is meant to be performed in front of an audience. Dramas are most notably performed as plays in a theatre though many dramas have been recreated and adapted for film, TV, or radio.

Photography

Photography is the art, application, and practice of creating durable images by recording light, either electronically by means of an image sensor, or chemically by means of a light-sensitive material such as photographic film.

Dancing

Dance is a performing art form consisting of sequences of movement with aesthetic and often symbolic value, either improvised or purposefully selected.

Music

Music is generally defined as the art of arranging sound to create some combination of form, harmony, melody, rhythm or otherwise expressive content

Rangoli

Rangoli is an art form that originates from in the Indian subcontinent, in which patterns are created on the floor or a tabletop using materials such as powdered lime stone, red ochre, dry rice flour, coloured rocks.
